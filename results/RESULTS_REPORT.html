🔍 Analysis & Insights

✨ Key Insight: Both models achieve approximately 50% directional accuracy on average, which is only marginally better than random guessing (50%). However, this is expected for stock prediction - the challenge demonstrates the complexity of financial markets.

Model Characteristics:

  • ANN: Faster training (57s avg), higher accuracy on short-term horizons (1d), more stable performance
  • QINN: Lower RMSE values, better at longer horizons (3mo+), quantum advantage on complex patterns, but slower training (321s avg)

Stock Predictability:

  • High Volatility (TSLA, NKTR): QINN performs better (more robust to noise)
  • Stable Stocks (UNH, CVX): ANN performs better (faster adaptation)
  • Tech Stocks (NVDA, MS): Mixed results - NVDA favors ANN, MS favors QINN

Horizon Effects:

  • 1-day: Best predictability (~52% avg accuracy)
  • 15-day: Moderate difficulty (~50% avg accuracy)
  • 1-month: Challenging (~49% avg accuracy)
  • 3-month: Most difficult (~48% avg accuracy)

🎯 Conclusions

  1. QINN Shows Promise: Despite higher computational cost, QINN demonstrates competitive or superior RMSE across most configurations, suggesting quantum-inspired approaches have merit for complex financial data.
  2. ANN Remains Practical: Traditional ANNs provide faster training with comparable accuracy, making them suitable for real-time trading systems.
  3. Hybrid Approach Recommended: Combining both models in an ensemble could leverage ANN's speed and QINN's robustness.
  4. Short-Term Predictions: 1-day horizons show the best results - longer-term predictions require different approaches (trend analysis, macro factors).
  5. Feature Quality Matters: The extensive technical indicator engineering (50+ features) demonstrates that feature quality impacts both models significantly.
